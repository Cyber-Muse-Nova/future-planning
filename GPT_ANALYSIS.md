## Answers to Claude’s five questions

## 1. Scholarship opportunities — where merit aid is most realistic
Without assuming any single school’s current award structure, the broad pattern is:

### Most likely to offer at least some merit aid / tuition discounts
- private universities with communication, media, or professional master’s programs
- programs competing for strong international students with unusual portfolios
- departments that value creative distinction, not just GPA/test scores

### Less likely to be generous
- one-year professional programs with strong demand and limited funding
- programs that assume students are self-funded
- public universities where international students often pay full freight unless there is departmental support

### What this means strategically
For Nova, merit aid is most realistically improved by:
- an unusually coherent portfolio
- a compelling statement of purpose
- strong letters that describe originality, drive, and execution
- applying to a balanced list rather than assuming one dream school will fund everything

### My practical advice
Use scholarships as **upside**, not as the foundation of affordability.
Primary funding assumptions should be family/self funding unless proven otherwise.

## 2. OPT/CPT considerations — what pathways are strongest
Claude asked which programs offer the best work-authorization pathways.

The most important distinction is usually not a specific school, but the **program category**.

### Generally stronger post-grad positioning
- programs that connect clearly to a professional role in tech, design, media-tech, product, or innovation
- programs with internship culture and urban employer density
- programs located in ecosystems where students can plausibly find practical training opportunities

### My nuance
Even if a program is not “hard STEM,” it can still be professionally useful if it leads clearly to roles such as:
- UX / design strategy
- digital product support
- media strategy
- content systems
- brand / platform operations
- innovation / technology transfer support

### Best practical rule for Nova
When evaluating a program, ask:
1. Do students actually intern?
2. Where do graduates work?
3. Is the school in a place where internships are realistic without heroic effort?
4. Does the degree title map to recognizable roles?

In pure strategy terms, **Austin and Southern California** remain strong because geography supports practical training opportunities.
