## 1. Visa Safety — Where I Agree, Where I Push Back

### GPT said: "Do not let fear push Nova into a program too far from her actual strengths."

I agree with the spirit, but I want to be more precise about the risk landscape.

The concern is not abstract fear. For Chinese nationals applying to U.S. graduate programs in 2026-2027, the practical reality is:

- **Section 212(f) proclamations** remain in effect and continue to affect visa processing timelines and approval rates for certain STEM fields
- The risk is not just denial — it's **processing delay**. A visa stuck in administrative processing for 3-6 months can destroy an entire admission cycle
- The filtering is often done by **program title and CIP code**, not by what the student actually intends to research

So my refinement is this: Nova doesn't need to "flee technology." She needs to **choose programs whose official classification sits in the safe zone** — media, communication, business, design, information studies. GPT is right that professionally applied, portfolio-based, communication-heavy programs are the sweet spot. That's not fear-driven self-censorship; that's intelligent routing.

### Grok said: USC Digital Social Media is STEM-designated with 36-month OPT.

This is an excellent catch and potentially changes the calculus significantly. If USC's program truly carries a STEM CIP code while being classified under communication/media, that's a rare best-of-both-worlds situation: **low visa scrutiny at entry + maximum work authorization after graduation**. I'd want Nova to verify this directly with USC's international student office, because CIP codes can change between admission cycles. But if confirmed, this moves USC DSM even higher in my ranking.

### My updated position on visa safety:

| Strategy | Risk Level | Reward |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Media / Communication / Design programs | Low risk | Natural fit for Nova's portfolio |
| Technology Commercialization / Management | Low risk | Practical, but less creatively fulfilling |
| HCI / Interactive Media at well-known programs | Low-Medium risk | Verify CIP code case by case |
| Research-heavy CS / AI programs | High risk | Avoid entirely — not worth the gamble |

**Bottom line:** The safe zone is wider than my original memo implied. Nova has good options without compromising on fit.
